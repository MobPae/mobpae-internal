# Product Overview

## Tagline

**MobPae — Your Trusted Financial Partner**

---

## What We Do

MobPae is a **earned wage access (EWA)** platform that allows salaried employees to withdraw a portion of their earned salary before payday. Unlike personal loans or credit cards, MobPae advances are:

- Backed by the employer (employer approves the advance)
- Recovered automatically from the next salary (no EMIs, no chasing)
- Interest-bearing only on the amount that exceeds the interest-free threshold
- Transparent — employees see the exact amount they will repay before confirming

---

## The Problem We Solve

**For employees:** An unexpected expense (medical, rent, travel, family) mid-month has no good options. Personal loans take days to approve. Credit cards carry high revolving interest. Borrowing from family is awkward. MobPae gives access to money the employee has already earned.

**For employers:** Financial stress in the workforce directly reduces productivity and increases attrition. Offering MobPae costs the employer nothing and is a meaningful employee welfare benefit.

**For NBFCs (future):** The employer-backed model dramatically reduces default risk because repayment is deducted from salary automatically. This makes MobPae's loan book more attractive for NBFC co-lending.

## Business Model

### Revenue Stream 1 — Platform Fee
- **₹175 per successful salary advance** (charged to the employee after employer approval)
- Paid via Razorpay before the advance enters admin review
- Can be waived by admin for specific applications

### Revenue Stream 2 — Interest on Employer Override (Commercial Agreement)
- If an employer approves an advance amount **above** the admin-approved limit (via their override percentage), the employer bears the **36% annual interest** on the extra amount
- This is a B2B commercial arrangement between MobPae and the employer

### Future Revenue
- Interest income from co-lending with NBFC partners
- Employer SaaS fee (under consideration)

---

## What MobPae is NOT

- Not a payday loan or predatory lending product
- Not a credit card alternative
- Not a recurring loan — each advance is standalone, recovered in the next salary cycle
- Not a debt product — employees access money they have already earned
